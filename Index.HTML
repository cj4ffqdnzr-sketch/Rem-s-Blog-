The evidence? A kitchen table. An orange Dutch national jersey. Two best mates locked in a moment of pure, chaotic, un-photographable brotherhood that somehow got photographed anyway. One friend down, two legends holding the fort, all three absolutely losing their minds with laughter.

Carnaval. Need I Say More.

Exhibit D — Full carnaval deployment. Family awesomeness: confirmed.

I've been to 83 countries. I've attended galas, premieres, exclusive rooftop events where the password was just a wink and a tailored lapel. But nothing — nothing — compares to a Dutchman at carnaval.

There's Rem, scarves and badges and full carnaval regalia, beaming in front of a tavern with his family. The older generation showing the younger how it's done. All of them holding drinks. All of them absolutely delighted to be alive. This is cultural mastery.

Most people learn awesomeness. Rem inherited it. Look at that family. Look at that energy. The Dutch know something the rest of us are still figuring out: that life is short, scarves should be colorful, and every occasion is worth celebrating at full volume.
